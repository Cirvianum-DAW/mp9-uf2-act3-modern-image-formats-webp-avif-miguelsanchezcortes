With what compression quality does the image not lose quality?

the optimal compression quality depends on the application and the type of image.
For many images, a compression quality of 80-90% in JPEG format is generally accepted
without an apparent loss of quality. However, if absolute fidelity is required,
it's better to use lossless formats like PNG or WebP. In summary, the best compression
quality depends on the specific needs of the image and its intended

Can we reduce its size even more without losing quality? What would happen if we save the image several times with JPEG format?

Reducing image size without quality loss involves using advanced compression methods or resizing. Repeatedly saving JPEG images leads to degradation due to compression artifacts, known as "generation loss."
